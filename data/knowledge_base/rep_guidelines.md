# Sales Representative Guidelines and Context

## Territory Management

Sales representatives are assigned to geographic territories to ensure optimal coverage 
and reduce internal competition. Territories are reviewed quarterly based on market 
potential, rep capacity, and account growth rates.

### North-East Territory
The North-East territory covers enterprise accounts in New York, Boston, Connecticut, 
and New Jersey. Reps here typically deal with financial services, insurance, and large 
media companies. The average deal size is $80,000-$150,000. Enterprise compliance and 
security certifications are critical requirements in this territory.

### South-West Territory  
The South-West territory covers mid-market accounts in Texas, Arizona, Nevada, and 
California's Inland Empire. The dominant industries here are logistics, retail, and 
e-commerce. Average deal size is $30,000-$60,000. Reps need strong knowledge of 
third-party integrations and API capabilities.

### West-Coast Territory
The West-Coast territory is the most competitive and covers Silicon Valley, Los Angeles, 
and the Pacific Northwest. It includes a mix of tech startups, healthcare tech, and 
media companies. Average deal size is $100,000-$200,000+. This territory requires 
deep technical knowledge and the ability to engage with technical stakeholders (CTOs, 
engineering leads).

## Quota Setting Process

Annual quotas are set in Q4 for the upcoming fiscal year. The quota-setting process 
considers:
1. Prior year actual performance (50% weight)
2. Territory potential based on TAM analysis (30% weight)  
3. Company revenue growth targets (20% weight)

Reps in their first year receive a ramping quota: 40% of full quota in Q1, 
70% in Q2, 85% in Q3, and 100% in Q4.

## Performance Review Cycles

- **Monthly**: 1:1 with manager reviewing pipeline health and deal progress
- **Quarterly**: Formal QBR (Quarterly Business Review) with manager and VP of Sales
- **Annually**: Full performance review including quota attainment, customer satisfaction, 
  and peer feedback

Quota attainment thresholds:
- Below 80%: Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) may be initiated
- 80%-99%: Standard performer
- 100%-119%: Strong performer — eligible for accelerators  
- 120%+: Overachiever — maximum accelerator commissions apply
